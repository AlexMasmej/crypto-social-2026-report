# pump.fun

## User thesis (Alex)

> _(reference included without additional framing — pump.fun as canonical Solana memecoin launchpad)_

## Research summary

Solana memecoin launchpad. Anyone can create an SPL token in seconds with no coding; tokens launch on a bonding curve and migrate to Raydium liquidity once they reach a market-cap threshold.

## Key facts

- **Launched:** January 2024
- **Chain:** Solana
- **Graduation threshold:** $69k market cap → Raydium liquidity pool (fixed amount of SOL locked)
- **Base trade fee:** 1% (historical; has evolved)
- **Volume:** became one of the highest-revenue crypto protocols ever through 2024–2025
- **Peak daily launches:** tens of thousands of tokens per day at various points

## Mechanism

- **Token creation:** free or near-free; user picks name/ticker/image
- **Bonding curve:** deterministic x·y = k-style curve; price rises smoothly with purchases
- **Graduation:** at $69k mcap, ~800M tokens deposited into Raydium LP with locked SOL — signals "made it"
- **No sniper protection** initially; bots dominate early mints
- **Fee accrual:** 1% of trades flows to pump.fun treasury

## Notable features / moments

- **Livestreaming feature** launched, then pulled after exploitative content
- **PumpSwap** launched as internal AMM to retain liquidity (reduce dependence on Raydium)
- **$PUMP token airdrop/sale** in 2025
- Continuously dominant Solana dApp by revenue for multiple quarters

## Social angle

pump.fun is not primarily a *social* app — it's a launchpad — but it's socially *generative*: every token has a Telegram/X, and pump.fun's UI surfaces holder counts and KOL-adjacent metadata. The *social* is in the metagame around the tokens, not in the app UI itself.
