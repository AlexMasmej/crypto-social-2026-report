# Believe v2 — Sentiment Markets

## Alex's framing

> **Sentiment markets: outcome-less prediction markets on the popularity of [Ben Pasternak], [Nikita Bier], etc.**

## Concept

Pivot from [[Believe (believe.app)]] v1's X-mention memecoin launchpad to **sentiment markets** — prediction-market-style positions on the popularity trajectory of named individuals, without a discrete outcome / resolution event. Not "will X happen by date Y" (moment-shaped), but "is person Z getting more or less popular" (trend-shaped).

Target subjects were **non-crypto tech-native figures** (Ben Pasternak, Nikita Bier — consumer-app founders and advisors, not CT personalities). The explicit goal was to **capture an audience outside of CT**.

## Why this is noteworthy for the wiki

- **Outcome-less prediction markets** is a distinct primitive: structurally a trend-signal market with prediction-market framing, without discrete resolution.
- **Target audience is outside CT** — an attempt to pull prediction-market behavior into a broader tech-culture audience.
- Sibling-in-mechanism to [[Breakout v2]] (Kaito Yaps mindshare markets) — both are continuous popularity/mindshare markets; Believe v2 just swaps Kaito's CT-centric oracle for named non-CT figures.

## Why it failed (per [[L6]])

Fails the same way Breakout v2 failed: **popularity is a trend, not a moment**. Outcome-less markets on continuous popularity have no urgency spike, so volume stays thin. The audience-outside-CT goal was orthogonal — even if Believe v2 had reached non-CT users, the market structure itself was trend-shaped and therefore structurally capped on flow. Bot-arbitrage risk is also high on continuous metrics.

## Positioning in the space

- Pivot within the [[Believe (believe.app)]] company, not a separate team
- Same category as [[Breakout v2]] (attention prediction markets) with a different subject pool
- Adjacent to [[Giggles]] / [[Frenzy (frenzy.fun)]] (also tracks trend-signal markets but on viral content, not specific people)
