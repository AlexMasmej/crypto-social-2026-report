# 2100 — research summary

> Primary source verbatim: **[[2026-04-20--2100-co-whitepaper]]** (full whitepaper text, 2019).

## User thesis (Alex)

> OG social using DAI interest.

**Note on the "DAI interest" framing:** per the whitepaper, the mechanism is DAI **staking**, not DAI **interest**. Users lock DAI (non-custodially — funds aren't lent) and receive username-tokens dripped per-block. The drip *looks* like yield but isn't — the DAI itself isn't yield-bearing in this mechanism. Alex's memory conflated "per-block token emission to stakers" with "DAI earning interest." Mechanism is now documented and clear.

## One-paragraph overview

A 2019 Ethereum-based protocol by **Citizen Hex Inc.** (@2100hq). Each Twitter username gets an ERC-20 with a fixed supply of 2,100. Users lock DAI to "stake" on usernames; every Ethereum block emits 0.00021 tokens per account (90% split pro-rata among stakers, 10% to the account owner, cap 100). Settlement on-chain on demand. Targeted private testnet Aug 2019, public testnet at EthDevcon Oct 2019. **No mainnet launch evidence.**

## Why it's interesting historically

- **~2 years before [[BitClout]]**, ~4 years before [[friend.tech]] — the identity-as-asset thesis was already articulated
- **Fixed supply per username (not bonding curve)** — different primitive than everything that succeeded later; arguably more fair, arguably less viral (no price-reflexivity loop)
- **Sybil-resistance for social graphs** was the stated goal — token-weighted subscriptions as a filter
- **Examples given in docs** (Snoop Dog backstage passes, Dollar Shave Club tiered rewards, KD collectibles) anticipated [[Farcaster]] channels + creator-gated content by 5 years
- **Explicit prior-art citation:** Hollywood Stock Exchange (1999), Stolen! (2015), Augur Twitter markets (2019) — the team understood the lineage they were in

## Why it didn't break out

- **Cycle timing:** 2019 was the DeFi pre-boom; consumer appetite for "tokenize a Twitter account" wasn't there
- **No bonding curve:** fixed-supply drip over ~4.75 years can't generate the speculative reflexivity that later SocialFi apps thrived on
- **Centralized off-chain issuance:** pragmatic but creates trust dependency; no trust minimization until on-chain settlement
- **Twitter API risk:** reliant on Twitter ToS / identity verification (same problem that killed many 2018–2020 "tokenize Twitter" projects)

## Key mechanism details (see whitepaper for full)

- **Lock DAI** non-custodially in Ethereum smart contract
- **Stake allocation** tracked in @2100hq's off-chain DB
- **Per block** (15s Ethereum at the time): 0.00021 tokens per username
- **Split:** 90% to stakers pro-rata, 10% to account owner (max 100)
- **Settle** to on-chain ERC-20 on user request (via signed authorization from @2100hq)
- **Name changes** mirrored from Twitter via signed broadcast
- **Opt-out** scrambles the token symbol via hash, halts staking
