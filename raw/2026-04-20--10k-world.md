# 10K.world

## Alex's framing

> **Watch-to-earn creator coins in a video app.**

## Parsing

Video app + creator coins + **watch-to-earn** mechanic. Viewers earn a creator's coin by watching that creator's videos — time-spent becomes the distribution surface for creator tokens. Creator value accrues from owning the attention mint.

## Positioning in the space

- **Inverts** the typical SocialFi dynamic: most apps ask viewers to **pay** for access (e.g. [[friend.tech]] keys, [[Zora]] content coin purchases). 10K ran the **opposite flow** — viewers are *paid* in creator coins for attention.
- Closer economically to [[Brave]] BAT + creator tips than to friend.tech — but attention-mining flows back to the creator's own token, creating alignment.
- "Creator coin = attention mining reward" is a clean primitive. The hard part: what gives the coin value on the other side?

## Why this matters for the wiki

Watch-to-earn is a **distinct third branch** in the SocialFi tree:
- **Pay-to-access:** friend.tech, believe.app, pump.fun — user pays for exposure
- **Trade-as-content:** Zora, Rodeo, Giggles — user buys/sells content positions
- **Earn-to-engage:** 10K.world, watch-to-earn — user paid for attention

Tracking "earn-to-engage" as a pattern is interesting because it historically struggles to escape velocity (Steemit, BAT, etc.) but keeps getting attempted.
