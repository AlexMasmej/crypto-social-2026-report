# CTA.fun

## Alex's framing

> **A Twitter account that auto-rewards $CTA to the most liked reply.**

## Summary

Not an app in the conventional sense — a **Twitter/X account with an autonomous reward rule**. When the account posts, the reply with the most likes wins $CTA tokens. A minimal, legible mechanism: the account's engagement directly distributes its native token.

## Why this is a good primitive

- **Zero UX overhead:** no signup, no wallet prompt to play — users just reply on X like they already do
- **Native to the attention platform:** the game lives where the attention is, not on a dedicated site
- **Self-reinforcing:** token rewards drive reply volume → more visibility → more rewards → more replies
- **Composable:** any X account could in principle run this mechanic; CTA.fun is the canonical implementation

## Positioning in the space

- Conceptual precursor to **[[Believe (believe.app)]]** — @launchacoin's "tweet to launch" mechanic has the same "X is the launch surface" insight; CTA.fun is "X is the reward-distribution surface"
- Simpler than [[friend.tech]] — no bonding curve, no chat-gating — just one rule: most-liked reply wins
- Echoes [[2100]]'s "owner claims reward by tweeting" pattern, but inverted (the account rewards others rather than being rewarded by others)

## Why this matters for the wiki

**Minimal viable crypto-social mechanic.** When you strip SocialFi down to its atomic element, what do you have? CTA.fun is one answer: *a rule that converts engagement into tokens, hosted on the attention platform you already use.*

Useful as a **concept article** in the wiki (e.g. `concepts/x-as-reward-surface.md`), not just a product article.
