# Showtime (tryshowtime.com)

## Alex's framing

> **NFT read-only gallery web app with Instagram-like UI.**

## Summary

Alex-built consumer NFT product. Read-only gallery experience that rendered NFTs in an Instagram-like UI — emphasis on **browsing/consumption**, not transacting. Essentially "Instagram for your NFT collection." Not a marketplace, not a minting tool — a viewer layer over the chains.

## Positioning in the space

- Adjacent to [[Zora]] (marketplace → social) and [[OpenSea]] (marketplace-first) but specifically **non-transactional UX**
- Bet on the hypothesis that **NFT consumption** (scrolling, discovering, appreciating) was an unsolved problem separate from trading
- IG-pattern mobile-web UX in a space dominated by crypto-native desktop marketplaces

## Why this matters for the wiki

Showtime is a useful data point on **"consumption-first, not trading-first" consumer crypto** — a counter to the "every post must be tradeable" thesis embedded in [[Zora]], [[friend.tech]], etc. The trajectory of "did consumption-first monetize?" vs "did trading-first monetize?" is a cross-cutting wiki question.

## Related

- [[Zora]] — eventually built a consumption-first feed on top of trading-first primitives
- [[Foundation (NFT marketplace)]] — curation-heavy NFT layer
- [[Rodeo]] — currently-closer to what Showtime's UX pointed at (feed of collectibles), but with trading integrated
