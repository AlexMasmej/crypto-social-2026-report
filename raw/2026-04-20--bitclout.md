# BitClout

## Alex's framing

> **Bonding curves creator tokens on their own blockchain using existing Twitter accounts to bootstrap the network.**

Three load-bearing choices in one product:
1. **Bonding curve** pricing for creator tokens
2. **Own blockchain** (not an Ethereum dApp)
3. **Bootstrap via pre-existing Twitter accounts** — tokenize first, ask permission later

## Summary

Launched as a public beta in **March 2021** on its own L1 (**BitClout**, later rebranded to **DeSo** — Decentralized Social). Every Twitter account in the top ~15,000 was pre-created as a **Creator Coin** — anyone could buy/sell coins for a given account via a bonding curve, and the account owner could "claim" their account to access accumulated funds.

Founded by **Nader Al-Naji** (a.k.a. "Diamondhands"), previously of the Basis stablecoin project.

## Mechanism

- **Creator Coins:** one per Twitter/BitClout profile, priced on a **polynomial bonding curve**
  - Formula (historical): `price = 0.003 * (CC_minted ^ 2)` — quadratic-style, cheap early buyers, expensive late
  - 10% of each purchase went to the **creator's "founder reward"** (a pre-set percentage at profile creation)
- **Own chain:** BitClout L1 with its own BTC-pegged token (**BitClout / $CLOUT / $DESO**) — users had to swap BTC → CLOUT to buy creator coins, creating a native-token demand sink
- **Content:** posts ("clouts"), reclouts (reposts), comments, Diamond tips — a full social stack built on top
- **Bootstrapping:** 15,000 top Twitter accounts pre-populated without consent; funds accumulated until the account owner claimed their profile by proving Twitter ownership

## Backers

Raised a reported **~$200M** from a remarkable cap table including **Sequoia**, **a16z**, **Social Capital (Chamath)**, **Coinbase Ventures**, **Winklevoss Capital**, **Reddit's Alexis Ohanian**.

## Peak + decline arc

- **March–May 2021:** peak euphoria. Major Twitter personalities (@balajis, @vitalikbuterin, etc.) saw their unclaimed Creator Coins trade at six-figure valuations.
- **Mid-2021:** **class-action lawsuit** filed over the non-consensual Twitter tokenization. SEC concerns over unregistered securities loomed throughout.
- **Late 2021 → 2022:** pivoted brand from "BitClout" → **DeSo** (Decentralized Social), expanded protocol scope to **NFTs, DAOs, Diamonds (tipping)** — less "friend.tech for Twitter" more "generic social protocol"
- **2022 → 2026:** DeSo persists as a chain with some consistent users, but never recaptured mainstream cultural moment. Effectively faded from consumer-crypto conversation by 2023.

## Why this matters for the wiki

- **Template for [[friend.tech]]** (and by extension most SocialFi apps post-2023): bonding curve pricing + profile-as-asset + token-gated chat is the BitClout blueprint minus the own-chain component
- **Bootstrapping lesson:** pre-populating accounts without consent **unlocked virality** (everyone had to log in to claim their own account) but also **unlocked legal exposure**. Every subsequent project has chosen differently — [[friend.tech]] went invite-only, [[Zora]] went opt-in post-as-coin
- **Own-chain lesson:** requiring users to bridge BTC → CLOUT added a point of friction that [[friend.tech]]'s Base-native approach (2023) eliminated. The industry's lesson: consumer crypto can't afford an extra swap step
- **Cycle-timing lesson:** BitClout launched at the top of the 2021 NFT/crypto bubble. Its peak valuation tracked the macro cycle more than product traction
- **[[2100]] → $ALEX → BitClout** is the 2019–2021 evolution of "tokenize a person": whitepaper → handmade → mass-market
