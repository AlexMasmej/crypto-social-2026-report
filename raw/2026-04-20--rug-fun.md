# rug.fun

> Third product in the [[Context (the team/company)]] arc. Gamified fair-launch memecoin platform on Base. Shut down with the company.

## Concept

**Launch-as-competition.** Creating a token isn't "launch, then hope" — it's a 24-hour competitive presale where only tokens that hit a threshold survive; losers' liquidity is reassigned to winners via an AI-run prize pool.

Framed as a critique of [[pump.fun]] style launchpads (where anyone can launch and most rug): rug.fun makes the rug mechanic **explicit** and turns it into the game.

## Mechanism

- **Presale:** 24 hours at a fixed price
  - Price: **0.000000004 ETH per token**
  - Supply: **500,000,000 tokens**
  - Required raise: **2 ETH in 24h**
- **Graduation:** tokens that raise 2+ ETH → "Launched" → auto-deposited into **locked Uniswap V3 LP** on [[Base]]
- **Rug:** tokens that fail → "rugged" → liquidity moved to the **Dotto Prize Pool**
- **Dotto (AI agent):** analyzes social engagement across launched tokens, picks one standout per week from the prize pool

## Key facts

- **Chain:** Base L2
- **AI agent:** "Dotto" — both evaluator and participant
- **Liquidity target:** Uniswap V3 (locked)
- **Team:** [[Context (the team/company)]] — Luke Miles co-founder credited explicitly

## Notable design choices

- **Rug is the feature, not the bug:** by making failure into a prize pool, the platform's economy feeds itself from failed launches (similar in spirit to [[Polymarket]]'s loser-funds-winner structure, but applied to launches)
- **AI curation:** Dotto's weekly pick is meant to concentrate attention on standout tokens — solves the "too many launches" problem that plagues pump.fun
- **Fair-launch framing:** fixed presale price = no sniper advantage, no insider allocation
- **Token supply fixed at 500M** — removes one dimension of manipulation
