# meme.market

> Fourth product in the [[Context (the team/company)]] arc. **Not a prediction market** despite the name — a platform where **NFTs were wrapped under coin "trends"** (per Alex's direct correction, 2026-04-21). Shut down with the company.
>
> ⚠️ The research below was compiled from web sources that mis-characterized meme.market as a memecoin prediction-market play. Treat the mechanism section with suspicion; the Alex-sourced framing (NFTs-under-coin-trends) is authoritative. Needs a re-research pass to replace the below with accurate mechanics.

## Concept

Trade predictions on meme-coin price movements — e.g., "will $DOGE hit $X by date Y" or "will $PEPE outperform $SHIB this week." Not the memes themselves; the betting layer **on top of** meme prices.

This is a conceptually interesting pivot: after building a memecoin launch platform ([[rug.fun]]) failed to break out, the team moved one layer of abstraction up — **speculation on speculation**.

## Mechanism (partial — research was thin)

- Prediction markets with binary / range outcomes on specific meme coins (DOGE, SHIB, PEPE, FLOKI, etc.)
- Real-time price data feeds
- Web3 wallet integration for on-chain settlement
- **Domain discrepancy:** user referenced "meme.market" but research surfaced "mememarket.meme" — may be the same product at different domains, or two different projects

## Key distinction vs [[Polymarket]] / [[Kalshi]]

- **Topical focus:** meme coins only, rather than general news/events
- **Audience:** presumably memecoin traders who already live in that attention cycle, not prediction-market natives
- **Chain:** likely [[Base]] (consistent with [[rug.fun]]) — unverified

## Why this might not have worked

- Memecoin traders already have **direct exposure** (just buy the token) — why add an abstracted binary bet?
- Prediction markets generally benefit from a wide topic set; niching to memes may have narrowed liquidity
- Regulatory exposure (prediction markets have CFTC scrutiny in the US)
