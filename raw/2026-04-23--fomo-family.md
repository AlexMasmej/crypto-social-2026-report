# Fomo (fomo.family)

## Alex's framing

> **Social trading app that's an easy-to-use cross-chain wallet with a built-in social graph. Trader-focused, not traditional IG/TikTok influencer. Has been winning and is doing $15M run rate — successful. Proof that crypto social so far failed and "social trading" is best. The best influencer type in crypto social are TRADERS, not traditional short-form video creators.**

## Product shape

- **Easy-to-use cross-chain wallet** — the transactional primitive is wallet-and-swap, not launchpad or prediction market
- **Built-in social graph** — users follow traders, not content creators
- **Trader-focused audience** — the graph is seeded with traders and the product is optimized for people who want to see/copy/learn-from trader activity
- Not influencer-content-first; the "content" is trading activity itself (portfolio moves, swaps, on-chain actions)

## Traction

- **Started 2025**
- **$15M run rate** as of 2026-04
- "Crushing it in traction" (Alex's read)
- One of the very few clear commercial successes in consumer-crypto-social post the [[great unwind]]

## Why this matters for the wiki

Fomo is the **cleanest counter-example** to the thesis that crypto-social categorically failed. The twist is that what "worked" is not traditional social-graph-around-content-creators (IG / TikTok / Zora shape) but **social-graph-around-traders**:

- The influencer economy in crypto is already concentrated around **people who make money trading** — their legibility/credibility/aspiration all trace to trading P&L, not to creative output.
- Traditional short-form video creators are not the crypto-social influencer class. Trying to build a crypto Instagram / crypto TikTok misses this structural fact.
- Social trading (copy-trade + follow + see-portfolio) is the product shape where crypto-social actually works because it **aligns the social primitive with the trading behavior the user is already there to do**.

This is also consistent with L11 ("crypto is for trading, not social"): Fomo doesn't violate L11 — it operationalizes it. It builds social _around_ trading rather than trying to redirect trading users toward a social product.

## Lineage / contrast

- **vs [[pump.fun]] / [[Bags (bags.fm)]] / [[Frenzy (frenzy.fun)]]** — those are trading products with some social garnish; Fomo is more social-graph-native while still trader-centric
- **vs [[Zora]] / [[Rodeo]] / [[Showtime (tryshowtime.com)]]** — those tried creator-content-as-coin; Fomo ignores content creators entirely
- **vs [[Believe v2]]** — tried to escape CT by targeting non-CT tech figures; failed. Fomo _stays inside trader-world_ but makes the social layer legible, which is the opposite bet and the one that worked.
