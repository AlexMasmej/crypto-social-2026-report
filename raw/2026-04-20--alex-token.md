# $ALEX token (2020)

## Alex's framing

> A **personal human IPO of $20K** against 3-year worth of future earnings. Then a **control-my-life experiment** where 1 ALEX = 1 vote in a snapshot-style way where people decided to vote on my life choices. Plus an **ALEX-holder-only Telegram chat**.

## Key facts (confirmed from contemporaneous coverage)

- **Launch:** April 2020
- **Raise:** $20,000 in **123 hours** from **29 participants**
- **Obligation:** **15% of Alex's income for 3 years**, capped at **$100,000 total**, distributed **quarterly in DAI**
- **Peak on-chain holders:** ~**200** on Uniswap
- **Peak market cap:** ~**$330,000** on Uniswap
- **Current utility (as of search):** holding **5,000 $ALEX** unlocks access to Alex's newsletter + holders-only chatroom
- **Contract:** `0x8ba6dcc667d3ff64c1a2123ce72ff5f0199e5315` on Ethereum mainnet (ERC-20)
- **Self-description:** "a blend between a small Income Sharing Agreement and a human IPO"

## Why this is historically important

- **April 2020**, which puts it **~1 year before [[BitClout]]** (March 2021), **~3 years before [[friend.tech]]** (Aug 2023), and ~1 year after [[2100]] (2019 — never reached mainnet)
- One of the **earliest successfully-executed personal tokens** with real cashflow backing — not a whitepaper like 2100, a live ERC-20 with actual income-share obligations
- Bundled **three mechanics** that every major SocialFi app since has used **one of**:
  1. Personal-token-as-income-share (IPO)
  2. Token-weighted life governance (snapshot voting)
  3. Token-gated chat/community (Telegram access)

## The three mechanics, unpacked

### 1. Personal IPO against future earnings (the $20K raise)

**Terms:**
- **$20,000 raised from 29 participants in 123 hours** (April 2020)
- Alex committed to **15% of all income for 3 years**, **capped at $100K total**
- Payouts **quarterly in DAI** (or equivalent stablecoin)
- Peak secondary-market cap on Uniswap: **~$330K** (so early participants could sell into a larger, more speculative pool)

Structurally: **tokenized income-share agreement (ISA)**. Holders got formal (not just implicit) claims on Alex's earnings stream.

This was notable because:
- ISAs are conventionally legal agreements (Lambda School, Pursuit, etc.). Alex tokenized one.
- Pre-dates [[friend.tech]]'s "buy my keys" by 3 years — but with **explicit cashflow backing** (quarterly DAI), not pure bonding-curve speculation
- The cap ($100K) is the critical design choice — it **bounds the holder's upside**, making it behave more like a debt instrument than an equity one
- The 200:29 ratio (peak holders vs original participants) shows meaningful secondary-market activity — people were trading $ALEX, not just holding the ISA

### 2. Snapshot-style life governance (1 ALEX = 1 vote)

Holders could vote on Alex's life choices via **Snapshot** (off-chain voting used by DAOs). Bigger balance = bigger vote.

Additional utilities bundled with token-holding per contemporaneous coverage:
- **1-on-1 sessions** with Alex
- **Network access** (introductions, etc.)
- **Possible seed round participation** in Alex's next startup

This is a **pure experiment** in applying DAO mechanics to personal life, not a copied pattern. No later SocialFi app has fully replicated it (it's deeply weird and hard to scale), though the *spirit* shows up in:
- [[friend.tech]] "key holders get Q&A access" (weaker version)
- [[CTA.fun]] "most liked reply wins" (engagement-weighted engagement, not life governance)

### 3. $ALEX-holder-only Telegram chat (token-gated community) + newsletter

Canonical primitive that every SocialFi app re-invented 2-3 years later:
- [[friend.tech]] keys = chat access (2023)
- [[Collab.Land]] token-gated Discord (tooling appeared after $ALEX's manual implementation)
- [[Unlock Protocol]] membership tokens (adjacent)

**Still live in 2026:** holding **5,000 $ALEX** unlocks the newsletter + chatroom — meaning this experiment has persisted **>5 years** as a functioning gated community, which is remarkable for a 2020-era crypto-social experiment.

## Positioning in the SocialFi lineage

The **missing link** in the wiki's timeline:

```
2019 → 2100 (username tokens, whitepaper only, never mainnet)
2020 → $ALEX (personal token, IPO + governance + chat, executed and still running)
2021 → BitClout (mass-market creator coins on own L1)
2023 → friend.tech (bonding-curve keys + chat access on Base)
2024 → pump.fun (bonding-curve memecoins on Solana)
2025 → Zora (post-as-coin, three-coin flywheel)
```

$ALEX sits between "idea" (2100) and "mass product" (BitClout) — the **handmade-prototype era** of personal tokens. Alex did manually what later platforms abstracted.

## Why this matters for the wiki

- **Personal authority** — Alex has first-hand, multi-year experience running all three mechanics (IPO, governance, token-gated chat). Future compile passes should extract his lessons into concept articles
- **Pre-platform era** — most wiki entries are "platforms enabling personal tokens"; $ALEX is one of the few "I just did it myself" instances, with 5 years of operating history
- **Governance-over-a-human** data point — almost nobody has run "snapshot voting over a human" at any scale; $ALEX is likely the longest-running primary example
- **Longitudinal data** — because $ALEX is still live, we can track actual long-term outcomes of personal-token economics (retention, secondary trading, utility decay) that no 2023-era SocialFi app has run long enough to test
