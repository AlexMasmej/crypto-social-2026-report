# Giggles

## User thesis (Alex)

> Same as frenzy but a TikTok clone with video scrolling, **not just a trading interface**.

The load-bearing observation: Giggles goes further than [[Frenzy (frenzy.fun)]] by **making the video feed itself the product surface**, not just the trading UI. Users don't look up a trend in a list and trade it — they scroll short-form video and trade individual clips.

## Research summary

Meme prediction market where **individual short videos become tradable assets**. Users post clips; others invest "aura points" early on content they predict will spread; early investors profit when a video goes viral.

Founded by **Justin Jin**, a **19-year-old Minecraft YouTuber**. Pre-seed round of $1,234,567 from **1kx Capital** announced **April 7, 2026** (the dollar amount is a joke — a "12345..." gag).

## Key facts

- **Status:** invite-only beta (as of April 2026)
- **Waitlist:** 450,000 signups
- **Funding:** $1.2M pre-seed from 1kx, April 7, 2026
- **Founder:** Justin Jin (19, Minecraft YouTube background)
- **Current asset:** "aura points" (fictional / in-app currency); real crypto rollout planned
- **iOS App Store listing:** "Giggles: Be Early"
- **Product goal:** become the first crypto app to cross **30 minutes/day** of user time

## Mechanism

- Users post short-form videos
- Viewers "invest" aura points early on clips they think will go viral
- Viral clips reward early investors — a **prediction market on content virality**
- TikTok-style vertical scroll UX
- Future: replace aura with real crypto (token unclear)

## Name origin

From a TikTok meme: "bro got banned from google giggles" — posted in reply to stale/unfunny memes, implying the poster is being fed jokes via "google giggles" and has been banned for lameness.

## Key distinction vs Frenzy and others

| Dimension | [[Frenzy (frenzy.fun)]] | Giggles |
|---|---|---|
| Primary UX | Trading interface listing trends | Video scroll, trading is secondary |
| Unit of speculation | Aggregate trend (e.g., a sound, a challenge) | Individual clip |
| Time horizon | Trend lifecycle (days–weeks) | Per-video virality (hours–days) |
| Target user retention | Trading session | Passive scroll session (30min/day goal) |

## Interesting mechanics

- **No memecoin launches** — avoids the rug-and-drain UX problem
- **Gen Z–native framing** — positioned as "first crypto app you spend 30min/day on," directly competing for TikTok attention share
- **Aura-first launch** avoids securities concerns pre-tokenization; soft-launch before crypto
- **Teen-founded** — rare and narratively powerful in a VC market full of ex-Coinbase founders
