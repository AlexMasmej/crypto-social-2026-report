# Context (the social feed)

> Part of the [[Context (the team/company)]] product arc. First product.

## Concept

A social feed that surfaced the **onchain activity** of people you followed — mints, trades, swaps, NFT purchases — as a readable timeline. Conceptually: "Instagram for your wallet's activity" or "Twitter for onchain moves." The premise: wallet actions are narrative, and seeing your friends' actions generates discovery + FOMO loops.

## Timeline

- **Founded:** 2021 (per shutdown announcement)
- **Status:** Sunset (team wound down in Q1 2026)

## Mechanism / product surface

- Connect wallet → follow other wallets → see what they mint, trade, collect
- Profile per wallet (ENS name, activity history)
- No native token; no creator-coin economy — an **aggregation + interpretation layer over public chain data**

## Why it matters

- Canonical "onchain activity as content" app — predated [[Farcaster]]'s Frames and [[Zora]]'s feed
- Solved a real problem (chain data is hard to parse) but monetization was thin — you can't charge for rendering public data unless you own the attention layer
- Template for every subsequent wallet-activity-as-feed app (e.g. Phantom's activity feed, Rainbow's social moves)

## Why it didn't break out

- No owned liquidity → no compounding economic layer like [[Zora]]'s coin flywheel or [[friend.tech]]'s bonding curves
- Activity is public; data moat was weak
- The team pivoted toward products with native economic layers (mint.fun, rug.fun, meme.market, context.markets)

## Related

- [[mint.fun]] — Context's next product, with native economics (mint discovery + aggregation fees)
- [[Farcaster]] — captured the "crypto-native social feed" space with a protocol-level approach
- [[Zora]] — eventually absorbed the NFT-discovery adjacency via acquiring [[mint.fun]]
