# Bags (bags.fm)

## Alex's framing

> Bags.fm's main appeal was **Twitter username royalty routing** (over TikTok). Especially for **AI projects**, people would create memecoins **without consent** and route fees to the creator, then **beg for the official person — most ignorant there's a memecoin attached to their project — to "claim" to prove the coin is legitimate**.

This is the load-bearing observation and it's a big one. The product's mechanic *formally* looks like a creator-monetization tool ("creator earns from trades on their token forever"). The mechanic *in practice* is a **coercive legitimization vector**:

1. **Anyone** mints a coin attached to a Twitter handle (frequently an AI researcher, team, or project)
2. The attached target usually **has no idea** the coin exists
3. Coin markets itself as "backed by [project]" — tokenomics imply official-adjacency even though it isn't
4. **Speculators demand the target claim** to prove the coin is real
5. Target is forced into a lose/lose: **claim** (validating what is essentially an unauthorized fan-token on their identity, possibly taking on legal/reputational risk) or **don't claim** (letting the coin trade on your name anyway, while looking absent or evasive)
6. Bags collects fees on every trade either way

Functionally: **a social-engineering lever applied at the identity layer**, not the token layer.

## One-paragraph

Solana-native token launchpad — anyone can launch a token without code, **attaching it to a target's Twitter username**. Trading fees auto-route to a virtual wallet under that username. The target can "claim" it later to access accumulated fees; prior to claiming, the association itself generates market pressure on the target to engage. Tagline: "Launch something new and get funded." TikTok fee sharing was added later; Twitter was the original and primary attachment surface.

## Why AI projects specifically

AI projects are **prime targets** for this because:

- **High information asymmetry** — most AI researchers/founders are not crypto-native and don't monitor Solana launchpads
- **High memetic surface** — AI projects have famous names, clear "brand," easy-to-parse cultural references that make for compelling meme launches
- **Speculation loves ambiguity** — "is this the official AI project's coin?" is itself a tradeable question; the target's silence *increases* the speculation, not decreases it
- **Cross-industry coverage asymmetry** — crypto Twitter talks about it; AI Twitter mostly doesn't; the target often learns from DMs demanding claim
- **Claim asymmetry** — the target has **no good option**. Engaging validates unauthorized use of identity; not engaging lets the coin trade on your name. Either way Bags collects

This maps closely to [[BitClout]]'s 2021 dynamic (pre-created creator coins for top 15k Twitter accounts, pressure on targets to claim), scaled down from "top Twitter celebrities" to "anyone worth memetically attaching to." Bags is BitClout's identity-coercion pattern **applied as a launchpad primitive**, five years later, with AI as the most-targeted vertical.

## Mechanism

**Token launch:**
- Code-free launch on Solana
- **Fee sharing configuration** required at launch: fees routed to a wallet associated with a **Twitter username** (primary) or TikTok username (added later)
- Target's fee wallet accumulates whether or not they know / have claimed

**Creator earnings:**
- **1% of all trading volume — forever**
- Once the target "claims" (proves ownership of the associated social handle), they can withdraw accumulated fees

**Holder dividends:**
- If a token has **≥10 SOL** in unclaimed earnings, **automatic distribution every 24 hours** to the **top 100 holders** proportional to holdings
- Aligns holders with volume, not just price — turns the top cohort into paid promoters

**Chain:** Solana

## Positioning vs other launchpads

| | [[pump.fun]] | [[Believe (believe.app)]] | [[rug.fun]] | Bags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chain | Solana | Solana | Base | Solana |
| Launch surface | Web | **X tweet by launcher** | Competitive presale | Web, with **X handle attribution** |
| Consent model | Launcher is the creator | Launcher is the creator (via their own tweet) | Launcher is the creator | **Attribution without consent of target** |
| Creator royalty | None | 1% to launcher | n/a | **1% forever to attached handle** |
| Holder rewards | None | None | Dotto prize pool | **Top-100 holder dividends** |
| Coercion vector | Low | Low (self-launching) | Low | **High — claim-to-legitimize dynamic** |

The key axis **Bags uniquely exploits** is consent asymmetry at the identity layer. Believe looks similar on the surface (X-mention launching) but critically: on Believe **the launcher is the person tweeting**, so there's no consent mismatch. On Bags **anyone can mint a coin attached to anyone**.

## Why this matters for the wiki

- **"Tokenize without consent" lineage** — 2100 (2019, whitepaper) → BitClout (2021, executed) → Bags (2026, productized as launchpad). Bags is the **current apex** of this pattern
- **AI-as-target** is a new wrinkle unique to this cycle — the AI boom provides a massive surface of prominent identities who are simultaneously (a) memetically valuable and (b) not crypto-native. This is a historically unique alignment
- **Coercive claim dynamic** deserves a dedicated `wiki/concepts/` article — it's a reusable pattern, not just a Bags-specific story, and it will show up in derivative products
