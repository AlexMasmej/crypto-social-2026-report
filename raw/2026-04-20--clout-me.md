# Clout (clout.me)

## User thesis (Alex)

> Solana creator coins.

## Research summary

Clout was an early Solana SocialFi app from founder **Ben Pasternak** — users could buy tokenized versions of social-media profiles, echoing the BitClout → friend.tech lineage. Downloads spiked after the iPhone app launched.

Clout subsequently became **[[Believe (believe.app)]]** — same founder, same core "tokenize social accounts" thesis, evolved into a Twitter-mention launchpad. In practice, "Clout" and "Believe" are two phases of the same project.

## Key facts

- **Founder:** Ben Pasternak
- **Chain:** Solana
- **Lineage:** BitClout (2021) → friend.tech (2023) → Clout → Believe.app
- **Status (as of research):** superseded by believe.app

## Post-mortem (Alex-direct, 2026-04-23)

- **Launched:** late 2024 — the **earliest** of the post-friend.tech creator-coin attempts.
- **Chain / liquidity:** Solana / Meteora.
- **Mechanism:** trade creator identity itself as the only unit of content — no posts, no profiles-with-content, nothing else. Pure identity-as-coin.
- **Bootstrap:** **capped 8K presale by 100 participants**, which led to early people **100x'ing on their investments**.
- **What killed it:** a **massive pump-and-dump on Pasternak's own token**, after which the product flopped. Pasternak then pivoted into [[Believe (believe.app)]] v1.
- **Generalizable lesson:** creator coins on **SOL / Meteora** can pump higher than friend.tech-on-Base ever did (the curve + chain combination removes friction and enables sharper reflexivity), **but with no utility on top of the coin, even the bigger pump doesn't translate to retention** — pump intensity ≠ product survival. Reinforces L1 (curves alone don't retain) on a more aggressive chain/curve combo.
