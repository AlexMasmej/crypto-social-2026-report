# Breakout v1

## Alex's framing

> **Creator coin for college students.**

## Summary

A creator-coin product **narrowly scoped to college students**. Identity gating via college affiliation; each student (or select students) had a coin. Builds on the friend.tech / 2100 lineage but with a **closed audience** — presumably starting with one or a few schools rather than the open crypto-Twitter universe.

## Why the audience choice is interesting

- **Closed graph → higher baseline engagement density** (classmates already know each other)
- **Status dynamics built in** — college has natural social hierarchy, which makes "who is worth owning a coin in" legible
- **Lower scam risk** — identity is verifiable (dot-edu email, student ID) vs anonymous Twitter accounts
- **Natural graduation** — literal: seniors graduate, creating a time-based exit/transition dynamic
- **Precedent:** Facebook did the college-gated launch strategy; Breakout v1 was "Facebook + creator coins"

## Positioning in the space

- **Audience-constrained** variant of [[friend.tech]] / [[2100]] / [[BitClout]]
- Echoes [[Facebook]]'s Harvard-only launch strategy but applied to crypto-social
- Unique in SocialFi: very few projects have tried **closed demographic gates** — most go for maximum reach from day one

## Why this matters for the wiki

- **Network density thesis:** does a closed high-density graph outperform an open low-density graph at equal size? Most SocialFi bets open; Breakout v1 bets closed.
- **Demographic-native crypto** is relatively unexplored — worth a `concepts/demographic-gating.md` article

## Post-mortem (Alex-direct, 2026-04-23)

- **Campus launched:** [[UC Berkeley]] only. **~15 users**, invite-only, **never publicly announced**.
- **Second campus:** never happened.
- **Why killed:** the team reached a fork in the analysis:
  - If the students weren't famous, there was no speculator interest → no market.
  - If the students _were_ famous, investors preferred to **back their underlying project** — memecoins consistently beat "student-memecoin" on any open speculation field.
  - The only remaining path was **project coins** (tokenize the student's actual company / venture) — but project coins are **less sexy than memecoins** to retail and **require more legal work** (securities exposure). Not the shape the team wanted to build into, so they wound it down.
- **What it means for the primitive:** demographic-gating-of-creator-coins ran into the memecoin gravity well. The closed-graph bet doesn't fail — it loses to the open memecoin field on every contested dimension (sexiness, regulatory lightness, virality). To revive the thesis you'd need to couple demographic gating with something memecoins can't replicate.
