## Introduction

In 21OO, participants lock DAI to mint tokens that are linked to Twitter usernames. This process creates a unique token per Twitter account.

Each account is represented as an ERC-20 token and has a fixed supply of 2,100. Collectively, the top 10,000 Twitter accounts would create a supply of 21 million tokens: 10,000 non-fungible groups of 2,100.

> @andrewyang + DAI lockdrop = $andrewyang

The owner of a username on Twitter is entitled to **100** of their own tokens. A public marketplace of 'minters' compete to mint and own the remaining **2000** tokens.

To mint 2100 tokens, participants 'stake' the DAI stablecoin on the Twitter username of their choice.

Every block will generate **0.00021 username tokens per staked account** until the maximum supply of 2,100 is reached. Assuming continuous staking and 15s Ethereum blocks, 2,100 tokens will be emitted in approximately **4.75 years**.

## Motivation

2100 tokens have multiple forms of utility and create natural incentives for both minters and account owners. For example, 2100 tokens can facilitate a weighted (and **sybil resistant**) social graph.

When there is no cost associated with subscribing to a feed on public networks like Twitter, users can subscribe to an arbitrarily large number of accounts. This creates a problem for influencers and popular accounts because they can't prioritize or distinguish die-hard fans from casual fans or bots.

With sybil resistant subscriptions, influencers can do a lot more for their followers:

- **@dollarshaveclub:** Hodl at least 1 $dollarshaveclub and get a free razor every month!
- **@SnoopDog:** Get a backstage pass with 10 $snoopdog
- **@KDTrey5:** Burn 1 $kdtrey5 to get a Kevin Durant collectible

## Context — prior art cited by the team

**Hollywood Stock Exchange (HSX)** — Created in 1999 by @maxkeiser and Michael R. Burns. Web-based multiplayer game; players use simulated money to buy and sell simulated shares of actors, directors, upcoming films, and film-related options.

**Stolen!** — Created November 2015 by @blader. Fast-paced mobile game; goal was to steal Twitter accounts from other users using in-game currency S-bucks which were earned by holding popular accounts.

**Augur Twitter Markets** — @AugurProject decentralized prediction market on Ethereum (founded 2014). In 2019, prediction markets on follower counts of Twitter celebrities became popular trading venues (e.g. @veil market betting on Andrew Yang's follower count).

> **Team's framing:** Stolen and HSX achieved finite success but suffered from the problem that their commodity had no utility outside of a single app. Augur Twitter markets gave the closest approximation of a platform-independent commodity, but exist only for a specific window and range. Further, follower count can be Sybil-attacked and isn't always correlated with increase in popularity/utility.
>
> 2100 proposes a mechanism to create **long-lived, platform independent, identity-linked commodities**. With the right implementation, we hope that 2100 will inspire a 'Layer 2' of utility.

## Philosophy

Long-term goal: completely decentralize the 2100 protocol. But current costs of decentralization (gas + UX) are barriers to widespread adoption. **First version of 2100 will be a centralized application with a deep Ethereum integration.**

- Ethereum used for non-custodial DAI deposits and for 'settling' assets as ERC-20 tokens.
- All other functionality orchestrated off-chain (open-source, auditable DB) by Citizen Hex Inc. (@2100hq).
- Plans to move on-chain if MVP is a success.
- Decentralized version would stake on a sidechain with a decentralized oracle for Twitter state.

## Creating Tokens

Token contracts are created when @2100hq approves that a string of lowercase characters is an existing Twitter username.

An account owner publishes:

> @SnoopDog: Add me to @2100hq

@2100hq verifies "snoopdogg" exists and signs an authorization message. Anyone can broadcast this to Ethereum to create the account token contract. Submitter earns **0.021 tokens** as reward for creating the contract.

## Minting off-chain

After creation, tokens are generated each Ethereum block by @2100hq in an off-chain DB. Not minted on-chain until settlement is requested.

- Users lock DAI in a **non-custodial** Ethereum smart contract. @2100hq never has possession.
- @2100hq credits users with staking allocation when DAI is locked; removes allocation when unlocked.
- Per block: **0.00021 tokens per username account**
  - **90%** pro-rata to stakers
  - **10%** (up to 100 total) reserved for the Twitter account owner (the "owner's reward")

**Example:** Alice stakes 1 DAI on $snoopdogg, Bob stakes 2 DAI. Per block:
- Alice: (1/3 × 0.00021 × 90%) = 0.000063 $snoopdogg
- Bob: (2/3 × 0.00021 × 90%) = 0.000126 $snoopdogg
- @SnoopDogg: (0.00021 × 10%) = 0.000021 $snoopdogg

## Settling on-chain

Any time, user can mint off-chain tokens on-chain as ERC-20s. App signs authorization message; user broadcasts to 2100 smart contract, which instructs the username token contract to credit balance.

Authorization messages can't be used more than once and must be signed by @2100hq.

## Owner's Reward

Owners get 10% of issuance (max 100 tokens). Until owner address is known, rewards issue to `0x000...000`.

Owner claims by tweeting:

> Claiming my @2100hq tokens 0xabcdef123cd516ecc3849daf6845e3ec868087b755

@2100hq verifies, signs authorization, user broadcasts on-chain to update claim address.

## Name changes

Twitter usernames can change. 2100 tokens must be able to follow. Anyone can tweet:

> The username @andrewyangvfa changed to @AndrewYang @2100hq

@2100hq verifies, signs, anyone broadcasts to update the token's name/symbol. The contract **address does not change** — close mirror of Twitter's rename.

## Owner Opt-Out

Owner tweets "Remove my username from @2100hq". @2100hq verifies sender, then broadcasts a transaction that **scrambles the symbol by hashing it with the current block number**:

> token symbol "snoopdogg" → "6455ffc29962d86b8b08c96b3db27ca0958a1bf1768ccecfde467d3b2bb0b782"

Staking halts. Existing off-chain token holders can still settle on-chain but no new tokens are generated.
