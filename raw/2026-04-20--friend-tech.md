# friend.tech

## User thesis (Alex)

> Discovery of bonding curve and L2s.

The load-bearing observation: friend.tech was the canonical moment the **bonding curve mechanic was proven to normies** and the one that put **Base L2** on the map for consumer crypto. Every subsequent SocialFi app either uses its mechanic or defines itself against it.

## Research summary

Social app where users trade "keys" (originally "shares") of each other's profiles. Each user has their own bonding-curve-priced asset; holding keys grants access to that user's private chat.

## Key facts

- **Launched:** August 10, 2023
- **Chain:** Base L2 (one of Base's first flagship consumer apps)
- **Backed by:** Paradigm
- **Invite-only** at launch — QR-code invite flywheel drove viral onboarding
- **Peak TVL:** $50M+ in early months
- **Airdrop:** FRIEND token claim May 3, 2024
- **Status:** effectively defunct by end of 2024; V2 shut down; team moved on

## Mechanism

- **Bonding curve:** price = `(supply² / 16000)` ETH per key
  - Supply is per-user (each subject has their own curve)
  - Early buyers pay low, curve steepens as supply grows
- **Trade fee:** 5%, split 50/50 between the subject and protocol (later changed)
- **Access gate:** holding a key unlocks the subject's private chat
- **Status surface:** your "portfolio" was public — other users could see whose keys you held

## Notable features

- Invite-code virality: limited-access QR codes driven by X follower count
- Identity-as-asset: the app tokenized attention on individuals
- Instant liquidity: no launch phase, no votes, no waiting — just a curve

## Why it died (hypotheses, contested)

- Reflexivity: curve-driven prices meant early users cashed out on later users with no underlying retention hook
- Chat product was thin — the access didn't justify the key price post-hype
- FRIEND token launch + V2 transition eroded trust; V2 didn't reach escape velocity
- Broader SocialFi winter in H2 2024

## Why it still matters

- **Template for SocialFi 2023–2025:** every bonding-curve social app since has been compared to it
- **Base's consumer moment:** friend.tech volume briefly rivaled major dapps, validating the L2
- **Mechanism lesson:** bonding curves alone don't retain users; need ongoing utility
